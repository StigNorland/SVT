# Can an SSV defect be assembled into the SO(10) 16? — Pass 5 (2026-06-05)

**Issue:** [#80](https://github.com/StigNorland/SVT/issues/80) Pass 5.
**Verdict: PARTIAL — decidable NO that the 16 "drops out", decidable YES on what it would take.**
The 16's internal arithmetic (multiplicities, anomaly cancellation) is automatic
once five binary bits exist, but two specific correlations the 16 imposes are
**not** supplied by SSV's independent topological sectors. Hitting the 16 is not
easy — it must be *arranged*, via two concrete new demands.

**Script:** `instruments/paper_ii/cp1_logse_16_assembly.py` · **tests:**
`test_cp1_logse_16_assembly.py` (10), with `test_so10_generation_embedding.py`

## The construction that makes it decidable

The 16 is generated by five binary coordinates `(c1,c2,c3,w1,w2) ∈ {±1}` with an
**even** number of `−1`s, and every SM charge is an explicit linear function of
those five bits (verified against the standard branching and against all four
anomaly coefficients + Σ Q vanishing):

| field | n_c⁻ | n_w⁻ | mult | binomial | T₃ | Y | B−L | Q |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ν^c | 0 | 0 | 1 | C(3,0)C(2,0)=1 | 0 | 0 | +1 | 0 |
| e^c | 0 | 2 | 1 | 1 | 0 | +1 | +1 | +1 |
| Q | 1 | 1 | 6 | C(3,1)C(2,1)=6 | ±½ | +1/6 | +1/3 | +2/3,−1/3 |
| u^c | 2 | 0 | 3 | 3 | 0 | −2/3 | −1/3 | −2/3 |
| d^c | 2 | 2 | 3 | 3 | 0 | +1/3 | −1/3 | +1/3 |
| L | 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 | ±½ | −1/2 | −1 | 0,−1 |

with
- **B − L = (c₁+c₂+c₃)/3**
- **Y = (c₁+c₂+c₃)/6 − (w₁+w₂)/4**
- **T₃ = (w₁−w₂)/4**, Q = T₃ + Y.

So "does a defect carry the 16?" becomes: can SSV supply these five bits **and**
the correlations the even-parity 16 imposes on them?

## What is automatic (no tuning)

- **(i) Multiplicities 6,3,3,2,1,1** are just `C(3,n_c⁻)·C(2,n_w⁻)` — they fall
  out of having a rank-3 colour sector and a rank-2 weak sector.
- **Anomaly cancellation** (grav-U(1), [U(1)]³, [SU(2)]²U(1), [SU(3)]²U(1), Σ Q)
  all vanish over the 16, recomputed directly from the bit formulae.

## The two correlations that are NOT free — the actual finding

1. **B − L is the colour trace, not a knot label. [NEEDS-ENLARGEMENT]**
   `B−L = (c₁+c₂+c₃)/3` is exactly the Pati–Salam *"lepton = 4th colour"*: B−L is
   the rank-3 (SU(4)) part of colour, *not* an independent U(1). Pass 4 guessed
   B−L from "Y-junction-present vs simple-loop"; Pass 5 shows that to reproduce
   the 16 the colour sector must **enlarge from the rank-2 SU(3) of #79 to rank-3
   SU(4)**, with B−L as the 4th-colour direction. A loose knot-type U(1) does not
   automatically embed in SU(4) alongside SU(3) — that is a real structural demand,
   and it relocates B−L *inside* colour rather than beside it.

2. **The chirality projection (16, not 32) ties two independent sectors. [NOT-FORCED]**
   Even total parity ⇔ **colour-sector parity = weak-sector parity**
   (`c₁c₂c₃ = w₁w₂`; the 16̄ enforces the opposite). In SSV the colour sector
   (Y-junction, #79) and the weak sector (CP¹ spinor, #78) are **independent**
   topological structures — nothing correlates their parities. So SSV as it stands
   predicts the full, vector-like **32 = 16 + 16̄**. Selecting one chirality
   requires a mechanism locking Y-junction parity to CP¹ framing parity, which does
   not exist. This is precisely the "two-for-one" Pass 4 *hoped* the spinor ℤ₂
   would give for free — Pass 5 shows it does not: the spinor ℤ₂ is a *weak-sector*
   parity, and the 16 needs it correlated with the *colour-sector* parity.

## Bit-source tally

bit ← CP¹ + winding/framing) · NEEDS-ENLARGEMENT 1 (B−L = 4th colour → SU(4)) ·
NOT-FORCED 1 (colour↔weak parity → chirality).

## Why this is the right outcome (and why it confirms the intuition)

The working hypothesis was: *if SSV were correct, the 16 should be easy to hit.*
It is not. The 16's arithmetic is automatic, but the assembly forces two demands
that the scalar core + #78 spinor + #79 Y-junction do **not** supply:
**(a)** colour = SU(4) Pati–Salam with B−L as the 4th colour, and **(b)** a parity
lock between the Y-junction and the spinor framing to pick one chirality.

This **sharpens** the Pass-5 programme rather than killing it. "Build a minimal
CP¹ LogSE" is now two falsifiable sub-questions:
1. Does a minimal multi-component LogSE whose colour sector is SU(4) (not SU(3))
   put B−L = 4th colour for free?
2. Does its defect spectrum lock Y-junction parity to spinor-framing parity,
   yielding the 16 and not the 32?

If a single construction forces **both**, that is the first genuinely
over-constrained SSV hit. If it cannot, the scalar core needs replacing, not
patching — and we will know which, decidably.
