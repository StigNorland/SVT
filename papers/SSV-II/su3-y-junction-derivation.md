# Does the SSV Y-junction phase-balance close to SU(3)? — PARTIAL (2026-06-05)

**Issue:** [#79](https://github.com/StigNorland/SVT/issues/79) (under #29).
**Verdict: PARTIAL.** The static phase-balanced trivalent Ψ-junction realises
the **maximal torus U(1)² and Weyl group S₃ of SU(3)** — but **not** the six
off-diagonal generators. SSV's colour phase-balance is the Cartan + Weyl skeleton
of SU(3), not the full continuous group.

**Script:** `src/paper_ii/su3_y_junction.py` · **tests:** `test_su3_y_junction.py`
(8 pass, incl. the control that the full Gell-Mann set closes to dim-8 su(3)).

## Task 1 — state space

Three legs meet at a trivalent vertex, each carrying a U(1) phase `θ_i`, subject
to the Kirchhoff phase-balance (circulation-conservation) condition
`θ₁ + θ₂ + θ₃ = 0 (mod 2π)`. Three phases minus one constraint → the balanced
configuration space is the **2-torus T²**, of dimension **2 = rank SU(3)**
(verified numerically).

## Task 2 — admissible moves on a *static* junction

1. **Continuous:** relative-phase rotations preserving the balance constraint.
   Their generators are the traceless diagonal Hermitian matrices
   `diag(a₁,a₂,a₃)` with `Σaᵢ = 0` — spanned by `λ₃` and `λ₈`.
2. **Discrete:** leg permutations (Schiller's R₃ "slides") — the 6 elements
   permuting the three legs.

## Task 3 — the algebra these generate

### The exact correspondence

The maximal torus of SU(3) is, by definition,
```
T = { diag(e^{iφ₁}, e^{iφ₂}, e^{iφ₃}) : φ₁+φ₂+φ₃ = 0 },
```
which is **identical** to the balanced phase space (`θᵢ = φᵢ`). The leg
permutations act on `T` by permuting `(φ₁,φ₂,φ₃)` — and the Weyl group of SU(3)
is precisely `W = N(T)/T = S₃`, the permutations of the three torus coordinates.

So the Y-junction realises the **normaliser of the maximal torus**,
`N(T) = T ⋊ S₃`: two continuous Cartan directions plus the discrete Weyl group.
Verified numerically:

| quantity | value |
|---|---|
| balanced phase-space dimension | 2 (= rank SU(3)) |
| Lie algebra generated by the continuous moves | **dim 2, abelian** (Cartan `u(1)²`) |
| su(3) dimension | 8 |
| Gell-Mann generators realised | 2 diagonal (`λ₃, λ₈`) |
| Gell-Mann generators missing | 6 off-diagonal (`λ₁,λ₂,λ₄,λ₅,λ₆,λ₇`) |
| leg permutations form S₃ = Weyl(SU(3)) | yes |
| permutations act as the Weyl group on the Cartan | yes |

### Why the six off-diagonal generators are absent

The missing generators are the root vectors `E_{±α}` (the off-diagonal Gell-Mann
matrices). Each one **continuously rotates one colour leg into another** —
`E₁₂` moves amplitude from leg 1 into leg 2. On a *fixed spatial junction* there
is no such continuous internal rotation: turning "filament 1" into a superposition
of filaments 2 and 3 is **physical transport / reconnection**, which is the SU(2)
*weak* channel (Schiller's R₂ poke, Paper II §15), not an internal symmetry of a
static colour vertex. The phase d.o.f. are abelian; they can only **permute** the
legs discretely (Weyl), never continuously interpolate between them.

## What full SU(3) would require

The same kind of extra structure that the muon no-go (#78) and the HQV/spinor
memos identified: the three legs would have to carry a genuine **ℂ³ internal
multiplet** at the junction — a 3-component order parameter supporting continuous
unitary mixing — rather than three scalar U(1) phases. That is exactly the
non-abelian promotion of the order parameter (cf. the CP¹/spinor scoping memo
`papers/SSV-I/muon-task-a-spinor-scoping-memo.md`). With scalar Ψ, the junction
gives the Cartan torus + Weyl group and no more.

## Interpretation — a consistent pattern across SSV

This mirrors the lepton result: SSV's **abelian (U(1) phase)** order parameter
generates the **abelian / Cartan + discrete-Weyl** skeleton of the gauge groups,
but not their full non-abelian content.

- Leptons (#78): U(1) winding → integer rungs + discrete structure, **no**
  continuous ℤ₂/spinor half-integer Berry phase.
- Colour (#79): U(1) phase-balance → Cartan `u(1)²` + Weyl `S₃`, **no** continuous
  off-diagonal SU(3).

Both gaps are closed by the *same* missing ingredient — a non-abelian
(multi-component) order parameter. The three native colour **labels** SSV already
has are the weights of the SU(3) fundamental's Cartan torus; what is not derived
from the scalar phase-balance is the continuous colour-rotation that connects
them.

precise obstruction, not a refutation: SSV's three-colour bookkeeping is the
maximal torus of SU(3), which is the right place to sit, but the gauge group
