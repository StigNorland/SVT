\begin{abstract}
This paper develops gravity in the Saturated Superfluid Vacuum (SSV) framework as a
mechanical consequence of gradients in local update capacity. The central idea is that motion
and interaction are not independent processes imposed on an inert background, but competing
uses of the same local dynamical bandwidth of the vacuum medium. Where matter loads the
medium with internal interaction, less update capacity remains for free propagation. Spatial
gradients in this remaining capacity bend trajectories and appear as gravitational attraction.

The aim of the paper is to state this mechanism clearly and narrowly. We focus on the relation
between local change-rate, time-dilation gradients, pressure gradients, and path bending. We
do not attempt here a full black-hole ontology, cosmology, or galactic phenomenology.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This paper develops gravity in SSV as a mechanical consequence of local update-capacity
gradients. The main question is not how to quantize a pre-existing gravitational field, but how
gravitational behavior arises if the vacuum is a real medium whose local rate of change is
finite and load-dependent.

\section{Local Change-Rate and Dynamical Bandwidth}

Introduce the idea that proper time corresponds to local change-rate and that internal
interaction and translational propagation are two uses of the same local dynamical capacity.

\section{Gravity as Refraction Through Loaded Vacuum}

Develop the core mechanical picture: matter loads the medium, reduces available propagation
capacity nearby, and creates a gradient through which wavefronts and trajectories bend.

\section{Time-Dilation Gradients}

Connect the gravity mechanism to time-dilation language and clarify how reduced update
capacity appears as slower local change.

\section{Consequences}

Discuss the intended scope of the mechanism for:
\begin{itemize}
  \item free fall,
  \item light bending,
  \item gravitational redshift.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion and Open Problems}

Clarify what is derived, what is heuristic, and what still needs formal development.

\section{Conclusion}

Gravity in SSV is treated as the bending of motion through gradients in the local update
capacity of the vacuum medium.
