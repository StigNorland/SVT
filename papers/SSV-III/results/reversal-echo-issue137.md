## Numerics notes (new lessons, each found by a failed run, each guarded)

1. **Splitting resonance.** The split-step LogSE has a
   Weideman–Herbst-type parametric instability when the kinetic phase
   per step k²dt/2 reaches π/2 for modes that carry content: at N = 512,
   dt = 0.025 the dealias cutoff sat exactly on the resonance
   (k_cut = 11.17 vs √(π/dt) = 11.21) and the run blew up at t ≈ 30,
   energy ×100 in ~10 time units while ρ stayed bounded. Cure: pin the
   cutoff phase at π/8, i.e. **dt ∝ dx²** down the refinement ladder
   (deviation from the pre-registered "dt halved per refinement",
   recorded; decision rules unchanged). Pinned by test.
2. **Log floor at vortex zeros.** A raw 1e-300 floor lets grid points
   adjacent to a core zero acquire b·ln(ρ)·dt/2 ≈ −690·dt phase
   rotations (the #119 H8c amplification in real time). RHO_FLOOR = 1e-6
   caps the rotation, touches no point with ρ > 1e-6, and keeps every
   substep exactly unitary and exactly T-invertible.
3. **Imaginary-time relax destroys v–av pairs** (their winding is not
   topologically protected — total charge zero): the H7a preconditioner
   must be kept short (τ ≲ 1) for dipole configurations, and tight pairs
   (d ≲ 3ξ) do not survive it at all — hence the colliding-dipoles
   geometry.
4. **Coarse-grid mask shaving.** At N = 256 (dx/ξ ≈ 0.53) the moving
   cores' spectral tail at the 2/3 cutoff is ~e^(−k_cut·ξ) ≈ 2%, so the
   dealias projection bleeds energy/norm at the 1e-2/1e-5 level — gone
   by N = 512. This is the visible face of the projection
   non-invertibility that the refinement rule discriminates against
   genuine dissipation.
