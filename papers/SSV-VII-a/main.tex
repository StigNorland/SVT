\begin{abstract}
This paper develops the quantum-mechanical limit of the Saturated Superfluid Vacuum (SSV)
framework. The central idea is that quantum behavior is not a separate foundational axiom,
but the linearized or low-amplitude hydrodynamic behavior of the same medium. Quantization is
tied to circulation and topology rather than imposed independently, and measurement is treated
as an irreversible reconnection process in the medium.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This paper asks whether quantum mechanics can be understood as a hydrodynamic limit of the
same medium already used elsewhere in SSV.

Develop the intended low-amplitude / linearized limit.

Develop the measurement claim as a physical event in the medium.

\section{Discussion and Open Problems}

Clarify what is actually shown, what is interpretive, and what needs more formal development.

\section{Conclusion}

Quantum behavior in SSV is treated as a hydrodynamic limit of the same underlying medium.
