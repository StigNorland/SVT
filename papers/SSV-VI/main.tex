\begin{abstract}
This paper replaces the former Papers VI-a (\emph{Galactic Standing Waves
and Flat Rotation Curves}) and VI-b (\emph{Galactic Morphology as Overtone
Structure}).  Both were organised around mechanisms driven by the central
black hole (CBH): a CBH-anchored standing wave was to produce the flat
rotation curve, and CBH spin overtones were to produce the spiral arms.
Both mechanisms are falsified (issues \ssvissue{122}, \ssvissue{124};
falsification record in Appendix~\ref{app:record}), and one mechanism now
covers both phenomena, so one paper covers both subjects.

The surviving architecture has three parts.  (i)~Gravity is the gradient
of the time-dilation field (Paper~IV): the rotation curve is
$v^2(r) = r\,d\Phi/dr$, with the Keplerian limit near a central mass
recovered structurally.  (ii)~The source of $\Phi$ is the
\emph{intrinsic two-term structure} of topological defects, measured in
the LogSE medium (issue \ssvissue{119}, H7a/H8): a concentrated
healing-length core and an isothermal $\ell^2\rho/2r^2$ circulation halo,
whose Poisson integral is a logarithmic potential
$\Phi_{\rm h} = v_h^2\ln r$ --- a flat-curve tail with no dark-matter
particles.  (iii)~Everything else is \emph{real Newtonian self-gravity}
of the baryons: in pre-registered $N$-body experiments (200\,000 stars,
FFT particle-mesh, no black hole, no dark matter), the baryons-only disc
reproduces the missing-mass problem (declining curve), the same disc plus
the measured halo term gives a flat curve, and spiral arms and a bar grow
by swing amplification with no driver --- the Ostriker--Peebles
instability --- directly superseding the CBH-overtone mechanism.  The
combination of a flat curve \emph{and} growing arms constrains the halo
amplitude to an intermediate, maximal-disc balance --- a falsifiable
condition, not a free parameter --- though the halo amplitude's value is
not yet derived from the medium (the circulation-halo route to its
normalisation was falsified in issue \ssvissue{119}, H9).

The data comparison stands on M31 and on the SPARC sample (83 galaxies,
pre-registered battery): the \emph{pure} constant-$v_h^2$ form ---
extrapolated through the core region the H7a/H8 measurement never
covered --- is falsified there (not competitive with NFW; 21\%
inner-quintile overshoot), while the halo amplitude across the
population scales as $v_h \propto M_{\rm bar}^{0.256\pm0.019}$ with
0.14~dex scatter --- the BTFR-equivalent exponent $1/4$, parameter-free,
now the measured target for any entrainment derivation.  On M31, with
the measured baryons fixed, the flat plateau requires an additional
component supplying
$\sim$83\% of $v^2$ whose amplitude is fitted, not predicted.  The
spiral-structure section records that the pattern's pitch angle is a
winding (shear) observable: the pre-registered pitch--$Q$ trend of the
old dispersion analysis failed its numerical test (D4b).  All retired
claims are kept as an explicit falsification record per the programme's
negative-results rule.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%%──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The rotation curves of spiral galaxies stay flat far beyond the radius
where the visible matter would let them fall \cite{rubin1980}, and the
standard resolution is a dark-matter halo \cite{nfw1997}.  The SSV
programme's galactic sector originally proposed a different engine: the
central black hole.  Paper~VI-a modelled the disc as a planar soliton
phase-locked to a standing wave at the CBH eigenfrequency, producing a
Mestel surface density \cite{mestel1963} and hence a flat curve;
Paper~VI-b read the Hubble sequence as the overtone spectrum of the same
wave, with CBH spin exciting azimuthal modes $m = 2, 3, 4$.

Both CBH mechanisms are now falsified, each by pre-registered
computation against its own decision rules:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The radial mechanism} (flat curve from the CBH wave):
    rebuilding the rotation curve in the correct framework --- gravity as
    the time-dilation-field gradient --- showed that the CBH frequency
    sets at most a wiggle \emph{spacing}; the flat plateau is a free
    amplitude that must be fitted to each galaxy, not predicted from
    $M_{\rm BH}$ (issue \ssvissue{122};
    \S\ref{sec:m31}, Appendix~\ref{app:record}).
  \item \textbf{The azimuthal mechanism} (arms from CBH overtones): it is
    contradicted observationally --- M33 has prominent arms and
    essentially no central black hole \cite{gebhardt2001}, the predicted
    Milky Way corotation radius misses by more than a factor of 20, and
    the mode amplitudes invalidly absorbed an astronomically small
    multipole suppression into their normalisation --- and it is
    superseded numerically: a self-gravitating stellar disc grows a bar
    and trailing arms with \emph{no} black hole present at all
    (issue \ssvissue{124}; \S\ref{sec:arms},
    Appendix~\ref{app:record}).
\end{itemize}

What replaces them is simpler and uses fewer ingredients.  The SSV
contribution to galactic dynamics reduces to a single, measured object:
the \emph{intrinsic two-term source} carried by topological defects of
the medium (Paper~IV; issue \ssvissue{119}, H7a/H8).  Its first term is a
concentrated core --- the Newtonian monopole.  Its second term is the
energy of the defect's quantised circulation, an isothermal $1/r^2$
energy density whose Poisson integral is a logarithmic potential --- a
flat-curve tail.  Everything else in a galaxy is ordinary Newtonian
self-gravity of the baryons, which we compute with real $N$-body
experiments rather than ansatz potentials.  The central black hole
appears nowhere in the mechanism; at most it is an inner-boundary
perturber.

\section{Framework: the Rotation Curve as a Time-Dilation Gradient}
\label{sec:framework}
%%──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Paper~IV develops gravity as the gradient of the time-dilation field: the
local update availability $A = d\tau/dt = 1 + \Phi/c^2$ slows clocks and
bends trajectories, and at leading order all weak-field phenomenology
follows from the single potential $\Phi$.  For a cold circular orbit at
radius $r$ in an axisymmetric galaxy,
\begin{equation}
  v^2(r) \;=\; r\,\frac{d\Phi}{dr},
  \label{eq:vcirc}
\end{equation}
and the potential is the sum of the Poisson integrals of the actual
sources present:
\begin{equation}
  v^2(r) \;=\; v^2_{\rm bulge}(r) + v^2_{\rm disc}(r)
             + v^2_{\rm BH}(r) + v^2_{\rm halo}(r).
  \label{eq:decomposition}
\end{equation}
Three structural points, established in the issue-\ssvissue{122} rebuild
(\ssvfile{rotation_curve_timedilation}, with tests):

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The Keplerian limit is recovered, not bolted on.}  Near a
    dominant central mass, $\Phi \to -GM/r$ and
    \eqref{eq:vcirc} gives $v\sqrt{r} = \sqrt{GM}$ constant --- the
    weak-field limit of the time-dilation field, verified in the test
    suite.
  \item \textbf{Each term is a Poisson integral of a real source.}  The
    bulge and disc terms come from the measured baryonic distributions;
    the BH term is $GM_{\rm BH}/r$; the halo term is the Poisson
    integral of the intrinsic circulation-energy density of
    \S\ref{sec:source}.
  \item \textbf{No standing-wave amplitude appears.}  The former VI-a
    wave term survives only as a falsified candidate for the halo slot
    (Appendix~\ref{app:record}): its amplitude was a free
    dark-matter-equivalent, and its frequency content set only a wiggle
    spacing.
\end{itemize}

\section{The Two-Term Source}
\label{sec:source}
%%──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The SSV-specific input is a measured property of topological defects in
the logarithmic-Schr\"odinger medium (Paper~I ontology; Paper~IV
gravity).  In pre-registered relaxation experiments
(issue \ssvissue{119}, H7a and the H8 cementing battery), an isolated
quantised vortex of winding $\ell$ carries:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{a concentrated core} on the healing length --- under the
    Poisson reading $\nabla^2\Phi = 4\pi G\,e/c^2$ this is the Newtonian
    monopole of the defect's mass-energy;
  \item \textbf{an isothermal circulation halo}: the kinetic energy
    density of the quantised circulation,
    \begin{equation}
      e(r) \;=\; \frac{\ell^2 \rho}{2\,r^2},
      \label{eq:halo_energy}
    \end{equation}
    measured as $e\cdot r^2 = 0.512 \pm 0.011$ against the parameter-free
    prediction $\ell^2/2 = 0.5$ at $\ell = 1$ (2.4\%), and
    $2.28 \pm 0.13$ against $2.0$ at $\ell = 2$ --- the $\ell^2$ law;
  \item \textbf{its own time-dilation field}: a Bernoulli density
    depression $\delta\rho\cdot r^2 \to -1/(2b)$, confirmed at two values
    of the LogSE coupling (0.6\% at $b = 0.5$; 11\% at $b = 1$).
\end{enumerate}

No radiation, no external drive, and no new postulate enter: the halo is
the defect's \emph{own} circulation energy.  As a Poisson source,
$e \propto 1/r^2$ integrates to
\begin{equation}
  \Phi_{\rm h}(r) \;=\; v_h^2 \ln r
  \qquad\Longrightarrow\qquad
  v^2_{\rm halo} = v_h^2 \;=\; \text{const},
  \label{eq:log_halo}
\end{equation}
the flat-curve tail, with $v_h$ the halo amplitude.

\begin{gapbox}[Open: the halo amplitude is not derived
(\ssvissue{119} H9 falsified the natural route)]
The pre-registered attempt to fix $v_h$ from the medium --- the
circulation-halo triangle $v_{\rm flat}^2 = G\rho_0\Gamma^2/(2\pi c^2)$
with the entrained circulation $\Gamma$ locked to the baryons' angular
momentum (Fall relation) --- \textbf{failed on both decision rules}: the
implied gravitational coupling misses by $\sim$35 orders of magnitude,
and the implied baryonic Tully--Fisher slope is $3/2$ against the
observed $\approx 4$ \cite{mcgaugh2012}
(\ssvfile{h9-circulation-halo-triangle}).  In this paper $v_h$ is
therefore an \emph{empirical amplitude per galaxy}, constrained --- not
fixed --- by the balance condition of \S\ref{sec:balance}.  Deriving the
entrainment law $\Gamma(M)$ (which must deliver $\Gamma \propto M^{1/4}$
at $\sim$17 orders below the baryon circulation, or fail) is owned by the
successor issue \ssvissue{129}.  Dispersion-supported dwarf spheroidals,
which barely rotate yet are dark-matter dominated, were the standing
discriminator for any circulation-sourced halo; that discriminator has
now been run (\S\ref{sec:dsph}), and it falsifies entrainment
proportional to the baryons' rotation.
\end{gapbox}

\subsection{Setup}

All claims in this section are backed by direct $N$-body computation with
\emph{real} Newtonian self-gravity --- softened $1/r^2$ forces from an
FFT particle-mesh solver with isolated (zero-padded, Hockney--Eastwood
\cite{hockneyeastwood1988}) boundary conditions --- not by ansatz
potentials (\ssvfile{disc_nbody}; GPU).  The disc is exponential
(scale length $R_d = 1$, $G = M_{\rm disc} = 1$), $2\times10^5$ equal-mass
stars, velocities initialised from the \emph{measured} total acceleration
with Toomre-$Q$-controlled radial dispersion \cite{toomre1964}.  There is
\textbf{no central black hole} (the M33-like configuration) and there are
\textbf{no dark-matter particles}.  The only SSV ingredient is optional:
the halo term \eqref{eq:log_halo} applied as the external acceleration
$a_h = -v_h^2/r$, switched on or off.  Decision rules D1--D4 were posted
to issue \ssvissue{124} before running.

\subsection{The maximal-disc balance condition}
\label{sec:balance}

The honest finding of the battery (rule~1: reported at full strength) is
that the halo-dominated configuration \emph{kills the arms}: at
$v_h = 0.65$ the disc is stabilised into featurelessness
($A_2 \sim 0.007$) --- the known ``dead disc'' effect of rigid halos.
Real galaxies have flat curves \emph{and} spiral arms.  In the 3D run the
combination is achieved at intermediate amplitude: $v_h = 0.45$ gives a
flat curve (outer/inner $1.06$--$1.10$) and growing arms
($A_2$: $0.004 \to 0.060$ and rising).

\begin{resultbox}[The balance condition]
The requirement \emph{flat curve and live spiral structure} forces the
disc to remain near-maximal inside with the halo taking over outside.
This converts the halo amplitude $v_h$ from a free parameter into a
falsifiable balance condition on every spiral galaxy: a galaxy whose
photometric disc is far from maximal yet shows strong arms, or a
maximal disc with no halo signature in its outer curve, breaks it.
\end{resultbox}

\subsection{Pitch angle is a winding observable, not a dispersion dial
(D4/D4b)}

the marginal-stability pitch relation of its Lin--Shu-type dispersion

the $m = 2$ phase-vs-$\ln r$ pitch, demand a monotone increase (Spearman
$\rho \geq +0.8$) wherever the arm amplitude is measurable
($A_2 \geq 0.02$).

\section{The SPARC Sample: the Halo at Population Scale}
\label{sec:sparc}
%%──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

One galaxy is an anecdote.  The SPARC database \cite{lelli2016} provides
175 galaxies with both the observed rotation curve and the baryonic
decomposition ($V_{\rm gas}$, $V_{\rm disk}$, $V_{\rm bul}$) at every
radius, and the SSV halo is unusually testable there: because
$\Phi_{\rm h} = v_h^2\ln r$ contributes a \emph{constant} $v_h^2$ in
quadrature, the model
\begin{equation}
  v_{\rm model}^2(r) \;=\; V_{\rm gas}|V_{\rm gas}|
    + \Upsilon_d V_{\rm disk}^2 + \Upsilon_b V_{\rm bul}^2 + v_h^2,
  \label{eq:sparc_model}
\end{equation}
with the SPARC-standard mass-to-light ratios fixed
($\Upsilon_d = 0.5$, $\Upsilon_b = 0.7$), has \textbf{one free parameter
per galaxy}.  The pre-registered battery (issue \ssvissue{133}; script
\ssvfile{sparc_halo_fit}; primary sample $Q = 1$, $i \geq 30^\circ$,
$\geq 10$ points: 83 galaxies) fits this against NFW (2 parameters),
MOND/RAR (0 parameters, $a_0$ global), and the endpoint-anchored
solid-body model of Flynn \& Cannaliato \cite{flynn2026} that prompted
the exercise.  Full numbers: result note
\ssvfile{sparc-halo-fit-issue133} and its receipt.
\textbf{No galaxy is hidden:} all 175 SPARC galaxies are fitted
identically and tabulated per galaxy
(\ssvfile{sparc-per-galaxy-results}), and the complete rule battery is
evaluated on three nested tiers --- the pre-registered primary sample,
$Q \leq 2$, and \emph{all 175 with no cuts}.  Every verdict below is
identical at every tier; the rule-(c) slope moves only from $0.256$
(primary) to $0.292 \pm 0.016$ (no cuts), remaining inside the
pre-registered window.  The cuts are SPARC's own quality
recommendations ($Q = 3$ flags major asymmetries; low inclination
leaves the velocity scale ill-determined), not a selection made after
seeing results.

\begin{resultbox}[Rule (c): the halo amplitude scales as

\subsection{The acceleration scale and the cosmological-horizon
over-constraint}
\label{sec:a0}

The constant-$v_h$ halo coincides with the deep-MOND tail with
$a_{0,h}\equiv v_h^4/(G M_{\rm bar})$ (issue \ssvissue{146}; script
\ssvfile{a0_from_vh}); the cored primary-tier value is
$a_0 = 1.13\times10^{-10}\,$m\,s$^{-2}$ (scatter $\sim0.31$ dex).

\begin{resultbox}[$a_0 = cH_0/2\pi$ from the de Sitter horizon, and the
duality over-constraint (H-A0-IR, \ssvissue{155})]
Papers~V's local horizons fix the holographic thermodynamics --- the
Unruh/Gibbons--Hawking factor $2\pi$ in $kT_H=\hbar g_H/2\pi c$ (S1) and the
area-law $\tfrac14$ (S2). Applied to the \emph{cosmological} (de Sitter / Hubble)
horizon, the same construction gives the IR acceleration scale
\[
  a_0 = c\,f_{\rm dS} = \frac{cH_0}{2\pi},
  \qquad f_{\rm dS}=\frac{H_0}{2\pi}\ \text{(the GH thermal frequency)}.
\]
This is not a coincidence of coefficients but the \emph{same computation}: the
identical finite-difference surface gravity used for Paper~V's local dumb-hole,
applied to the Hubble flow $v=H_0r$ against $c$, returns
$g_{\rm dS}=\tfrac12|\partial_r(c^2-v^2)|_{r_{\rm dS}}=cH_0$ (to $10^{-12}$) and
hence $a_0=g_{\rm dS}/2\pi$.  The \emph{magnitude} is conceded: $H_0$ (via
$\Lambda=(8\pi G/c^2)(P_0/\rho_0c^2)$ and the undetermined saturation pressure
$P_0$, Paper~VIII) is an input, just as $G$'s magnitude is (\ssvissue{154}); only
the dimensionless coefficient is tested. The battery \ssvfile{a0_overconstraint}
confronts the measured $a_0$ against each candidate (ratio measured/predicted,
$H_0=73$): $cH_0/2\pi$ gives $0.999$ (and $1.082$ at $H_0=67.4$ --- consistent
across the $H_0$ tension); $c^2\sqrt{\Lambda}$ gives $0.12$ --- \textbf{excluded
at $8.4\times$} (and excluded under the independent RAR scale too), a clean
negative for the cosmological-constant branch. The over-constraint is therefore
\emph{survived}: the local screens (V) and the cosmological screen carry one
thermodynamics; the otherwise-falsifier-free duality \emph{could} have been
falsified here (an $a_0$ on the $\sqrt\Lambda$ form would have done it) and was
not.

\textbf{Survived, not confirmed --- and $1/2\pi$ is not singled out (rule 1).}
Verlinde's $cH_0/6$ differs from $cH_0/2\pi$ by only $6/2\pi=0.955$ ($0.020$ dex),
far inside the $\sim0.31$ dex SPARC scatter ($0.065\sigma$). Worse for a clean
claim: the \emph{independent, tighter} radial-acceleration-relation scale
$a_0=1.20\times10^{-10}$ \cite{mcgaugh2016} mildly favours $1/6$ at $H_0=73$
(ratio $1.015$) while SSV's own halo $a_0$ favours $1/2\pi$ (ratio $0.999$) --- the
two best estimators straddle the coefficients ($\sim$6\% apart), and even the
RAR's small random error is blocked by its $\sim0.08$ dex systematic and the
$0.035$ dex $H_0$ tension. So $1/2\pi$ is SSV's prediction and is consistent, but
\emph{not} singled out by the data. The decisive separator is the
redshift-evolution falsifier (re-pinned from \ssvissue{146}, rule A4): the $cH$

high-$z$ rotation curves not yet in hand. Result note \ssvfile{h-a0-ir-issue155}.
\end{resultbox}

\subsection{The dwarf-spheroidal ledger: the halo reads mass, not
rotation}
\label{sec:dsph}

The eight classical Milky Way dwarf spheroidals barely rotate
($v_{\rm rot} \lesssim 3$ km/s; dispersion-supported) yet are the most
dark-matter-dominated systems known --- the pre-registered battery of
issue \ssvissue{147} (script \ssvfile{dsph_ledger}; McConnachie-2012
values, Wolf estimator $v_c(r_{1/2}) = \sqrt{3}\,\sigma_{\rm los}$)
confronts them with two competing sourcings of the halo amplitude:
the measured mass law of Eq.~\eqref{eq:vh_mbar} (model A), and
rotation-proportional entrainment $v_h \propto \Gamma_{\rm bar}
= 2\pi R\,v_{\rm rot}$ normalized at the H9 Milky-Way reference
(model B) --- the reading under which ``circulation halo'' is fed by
what the baryons are doing.

\begin{falsbox}[\ssvissue{147} B1: rotation-proportional entrainment is
falsified at the dwarf spheroidals]
Extrapolated \emph{three decades in mass} below the SPARC sample, the
dwarfs sit on the mass law: median offset $+0.20$ dex (per-dwarf range
$+0.01$ to $+0.43$, all inside the pre-registered $\pm 0.5$ window).
The rotation law's \emph{upper limits} miss by a median of
\textbf{2.7 dex} --- a factor $\sim 500$ --- and the verdict is stable
across the entire pre-registered robustness sweep ($M_*/L$ varied
$\times/\div 2$, $v_{\rm rot}$ limits 1--10 km/s, $\pm 30\%$ anisotropy
systematic; 27 combinations).  The halo amplitude reads baryonic
\emph{mass}, not baryonic \emph{rotation}.  Two honesty items recorded
with it: (i) the naive ``no rotation $\Rightarrow$ no circulation
budget'' killer does \emph{not} bind --- even at $v_{\rm rot} = 1$ km/s
the dwarfs carry $\gtrsim 10^{15}$ times the H9-inverted circulation
requirement, so it is the \emph{correlation}, not the budget, that
discriminates; (ii) the dwarfs sit systematically \emph{above} the
relation by $+0.20$ dex (they are MW satellites; the tidal confound is
recorded, and at face value the offset means slightly \emph{more} halo
than the mass law predicts, in the same direction the relation's
0.14-dex scatter allows).
\end{falsbox}

\begin{falsbox}[\ssvissue{147} B3: a universal kpc-scale core is
falsified at dwarf scales]
Under the post-hoc cored form with a \emph{universal}
$r_c = 2.5$~kpc (the SPARC lower quartile --- the choice most
favorable to dwarf halos), the predicted dispersions collapse to
$\sigma_{\rm pred} \approx 1$--$6$ km/s against observed
$6.6$--$11.7$ km/s: \textbf{7 of 8 dwarfs fall below half the observed
dispersion} (threshold: $\geq 6$ of 8), sweep-stable.  The \ssvissue{133}
promotion condition sharpens accordingly: any $r_c$(galaxy) relation
must deliver $r_c \lesssim r_{1/2} \sim 0.2$--$0.9$~kpc at dwarf scale
--- a single medium-set core length is dead; only a baryon-set core
scale survives.
\end{falsbox}

sense as $G$.

\section{Conclusion}

A galaxy, in the surviving SSV picture, is baryons under real gravity
plus the intrinsic circulation halo of the vacuum's defect structure.
Real gravity does what it has been known to do since the 1970s: it
declines where mass runs out, and it amplifies spiral structure from
noise.  The medium contributes exactly one new term --- a measured,
isothermal $1/r^2$ energy density whose potential is logarithmic --- and
that term supplies the flat tail.  The central black hole drives
nothing.  What the framework does not yet supply is the halo amplitude:
its natural derivation route was tested and falsified, and per the
programme's rules that negative is stated as the result it is.  At
population scale the picture sharpened in both directions: the pure
constant-$v_h^2$ form fails on SPARC exactly where it extrapolated past

follows $v_h \propto M_{\rm bar}^{0.256\pm0.019}$ with 0.14 dex scatter

sector's forward path --- the entrainment law that must produce that
exponent, the core-scale derivation, the Tully--Fisher
normalisation, the dwarf-spheroidal discriminator --- is owned by
issues \ssvissue{129} and \ssvissue{133}.

\subsection{The CBH standing-wave rotation-curve mechanism (VI-a)}

\begin{falsbox}[Falsified: the flat plateau is not produced by the CBH
(issue \ssvissue{122})]
The mechanism claimed a disc soliton phase-locked to a CBH-anchored
standing wave at $f_{\rm BH} = f_p\,(m_p/M_{\rm BH})$, yielding a Mestel
surface density and an exactly flat baryonic curve.  Rebuilt in the
correct time-dilation-gradient framework with M31's real baryons fixed,
the wave component must supply $\sim$83\% of $v^2$ with its amplitude
\textbf{fitted} ($V_w = 230$~km/s), not predicted from
$M_{\rm BH}/f_{\rm BH}$; the CBH frequency sets only the wiggle spacing
$\Delta r$.  A mechanism whose amplitude is free is a dark-matter
parametrisation, not a prediction.  Carried problems that die with it:
the calibration constant $\mathcal{C} = 1.8\times10^9\,{\rm kpc}\,
M_\odot$ was numerological (anchored to one galaxy), and the wavelength
scaling carried an unresolved $\lambda_{\rm BH} \propto M_{\rm BH}$
versus $\Delta r \propto 1/M_{\rm BH}$ tension.  The plane-of-satellites
reading (corotating dwarfs \cite{ibata2013} as a nodal sheet of the same
wave) was a corollary of the falsified wave and is retired untested.
The Mestel profile itself \cite{mestel1963} remains, of course, a
correct description of a $\Sigma \propto 1/r$ disc; what is retired is
the claim that a CBH-locked LogSE soliton puts the baryons in that
profile.
\end{falsbox}

\subsection{The CBH-overtone arm mechanism (VI-b)}

\begin{falsbox}[Falsified and superseded: arms need no black hole
(issue \ssvissue{124})]
The mechanism read spiral arms as CBH-spin-excited azimuthal modes
($m = 2, 3, 4$) of the VI-a wave, and the Hubble sequence as its overtone
spectrum.  Four independent falsifiers:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{M33}: prominent grand-design-class arms with
    $M_{\rm BH} \lesssim 1.5\times10^3\,M_\odot$ \cite{gebhardt2001} ---
    the predicted node spacing exceeds the size of the observable
    universe's disc analogue; the mechanism predicts \emph{no} arms.
  \item \textbf{Corotation}: $r_c = m\mathcal{C}/(\pi M_{\rm BH})$ gives
    266~kpc for the Milky Way against the observed 6--12~kpc ---
    off by more than $20\times$.
  \item \textbf{Multipole suppression}: the $\varepsilon_m(a_{\rm BH})$
    amplitudes absorbed a Hansen-coefficient suppression of order
    $(r_g/\Delta r)^m \sim (5\times10^{-10})^m$ ``into the
    normalisation''; the true spin-driven amplitudes are
    $\sim 10^{-19}$, not percent-level.  The percent-level table (and
    the pitch-angle--$M_{\rm BH}$ anti-correlation built on it) was
    unfounded.
  \item \textbf{Winding}: the pinned-refreshed-mode picture contradicted
    the pitch-from-winding picture used in the same chapter, and the
    proposed $J_m(kr)\cos m\phi$ pattern is not a spiral.
\end{enumerate}
Superseded by: \S\ref{sec:arms} --- swing amplification of the
self-gravitating baryons produces bar + arms with no BH in the
simulation at all (D3).  The pitch--$Q$ corollary of the surviving
dispersion analysis subsequently failed its own pre-registered test

\end{falsbox}
