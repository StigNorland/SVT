### 4.5   The Muon (µ± ): formulation of the 3D derivation

The muon is identified, not with a planar core-pulsation of the electron vortex, but with the
lowest bound oscillatory mode of the full 3D toroidal defect once the chiral-shear channel is
included. The planar Bogoliubov-de Gennes analysis of Appendix C already proves that the
standard LogSE alone cannot supply such a mode. For the axisymmetric amplitude channel the
effective operator is
\[
\hat H_{\rm amp} = -\partial_{\tilde s}^2 + \frac{1}{\tilde s}\partial_{\tilde s} + 4(1-f_0^2),
\]
with
\[
V_{\rm eff}(\tilde s)=4(1-f_0^2)\ge 0
\]
for all \(\tilde s\). The potential is therefore purely repulsive and supports no bound s-wave
breathing state. The muon cannot arise from the 2D core profile of the LogSE vortex.

The correct candidate is instead the major-radius breathing of the equilibrium torus,
\[
R_e=\frac{\xi}{\alpha}=\frac{\hbar}{\alpha m_e c},
\]
coupled to the transverse chiral mode generated by the additional Lagrangian term
\[
\mathcal L_\perp
=
-\frac{\lambda \hbar^2}{2m_0\rho_0}\,(\nabla\times j)^2,
\qquad
j=\frac{\hbar}{2m_0 i}\left(\Psi^\ast \nabla\Psi-\Psi\nabla\Psi^\ast\right).
\]
This is the first place in the theory where a bound muon-like excitation can appear: the torus
has a genuine geometric breathing coordinate absent from the planar calculation, while the
chiral sector provides the only available transverse restoring channel at the core scale.

To formulate the derivation, let \(\Psi_0(\mathbf x)\) denote the static equilibrium toroidal
solution and write the usual BdG perturbation
\[
\Psi(\mathbf x,t)
=
e^{-i\mu_{\rm chem} t/\hbar}
\left[
\Psi_0(\mathbf x)
+u(\mathbf x)e^{-i\omega t}
+v^\ast(\mathbf x)e^{i\omega t}
\right].
\]
Linearisation of the full action \(\mathcal L+\mathcal L_\perp\) then gives the 3D toroidal
eigenvalue problem
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
\hat L_0+\hat L_\perp & \hat M_0+\hat M_\perp \\
-(\hat M_0+\hat M_\perp)^\ast & -(\hat L_0+\hat L_\perp)^\ast
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
u\\
v
\end{pmatrix}
=
\hbar\omega
\begin{pmatrix}
u\\
v
\end{pmatrix},
\]
where \(\hat L_0,\hat M_0\) are the LogSE operators and \(\hat L_\perp,\hat M_\perp\) are the
quadratic fluctuations induced by the chiral-shear term. Unlike the planar problem, this
operator retains both torus curvature and coupling between longitudinal and transverse
channels.

The muon candidate is the lowest localized eigenmode with three properties:

1. it is axisymmetric in the minor cross-section of the torus;
2. it changes the major radius \(R_e\) and therefore represents a genuine ring-breathing mode;
3. it has nonzero overlap with the transverse chiral sector.

The importance of the third condition is fixed already by the scale mismatch in the uncoupled
problem. From the string-tension estimate of Appendix C,
\[
\omega_{\rm ring}^2 \sim \alpha^2 \omega_c^2 \ln(1/\alpha),
\qquad
\omega_c = \frac{m_e c^2}{\hbar},
\]
so that
\[
\frac{\omega_{\rm ring}}{\omega_c}
\sim
\alpha\sqrt{\ln(1/\alpha)}
\approx 0.016.
\]
This is far below the muon scale. By contrast, a true muon mode must satisfy
\[
\hbar\omega_\mu = m_\mu c^2,
\]
and therefore, in the normalization used here,
\[
\omega_\mu \approx 0.207\,\omega_c.
\]
The coupled 3D calculation must therefore supply an enhancement of roughly
\[
\frac{\omega_\mu}{\omega_{\rm ring}}\approx \frac{0.207}{0.016}\approx 13.
\]
This number is not a fit parameter; it is the quantitative target any successful toroidal
eigenmode calculation must hit.

The minimal reduced description is a two-mode collective-coordinate model. Let \(q(t)\) be the
fractional breathing of the major radius and \(\chi(t)\) the amplitude of the lowest localized
transverse chiral mode:
\[
R(t)=R_e[1+q(t)].
\]
Expanding the full action to quadratic order about the equilibrium torus gives
\[
S^{(2)}=
\frac12\int dt\,
\left[
M_R \dot q^{\,2}
+M_\chi \dot\chi^{\,2}
-K_R q^2
-K_\chi \chi^2
-2G q\chi
\right].
\]
The normal-mode condition is therefore
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
K_R-\omega^2 M_R & G \\
G & K_\chi-\omega^2 M_\chi
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
q\\
\chi
\end{pmatrix}
=0,
\]
or equivalently
\[
(K_R-\omega^2 M_R)(K_\chi-\omega^2 M_\chi)-G^2=0.
\]
This equation exhibits the central physical point: the muon scale can only emerge if the
ring-breathing mode mixes strongly with the chiral transverse channel through the coupling
\(G\). The quantity to be derived is therefore not the half-integer coefficient \(3/2\) itself, but
the coupled eigenfrequency of the toroidal defect.

Until that computation is completed, the assignment
\[
m_{\mu^\pm}\approx \frac{3}{2}\mu_0=\frac{3m_e}{2\alpha}\approx 105.038\ {\rm MeV}
\]
should still be read as a phenomenological rung of the \(\alpha\)-harmonic ladder, not as a
finished first-principles result. The derivation problem is now sharply defined:
\[
\boxed{
\text{Find the lowest bound eigenvalue of the 3D toroidal BdG operator derived from }
\mathcal L+\mathcal L_\perp .
}
\]
If that eigenvalue reproduces the observed muon mass, the half-integer rung is explained rather
than assumed.

### 4.5.1   Helicity bridge normalization

The reduced toroidal calculation identifies the relevant Kelvin sector not with the raw radial
and vertical displacement seeds separately, but with the helicity eigenstates
\[
K_{\sigma,\pm}
=
\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\left(
-\partial_r\Psi_0+\sigma i[-\partial_z\Psi_0]
\right)e^{\pm i\varphi},
\qquad
\sigma=\pm1.
\]
In this basis the spurious weak symplectic plane of the raw \((K_r,K_z)\) extension is removed,
and the current-curl bridge is Hermitian in the BdG sense.

The chiral-shear coefficient entering the projected BdG matrix is not the bare \(\lambda\)
itself. From
\[
\mathcal L_\perp
=
-\frac{\lambda\hbar^2}{2m_0\rho_0}
(\nabla\times j)^2
\]
the dimensionless transverse stiffness is \(\lambda m_0/\rho_0\). Paper I fixes
\[
\lambda {m_0\over\rho_0}=\alpha^2.
\]
The Kelvin modes live on the major ring while the breathing/chiral core modes are normalized in
the meridional cross-section. The projection therefore carries the toroidal scale factor
\[
\left(\frac{R_e}{\xi}\right)^2=\alpha^{-2}.
\]
The remaining order-one factor is the helicity/cross-section integral. In the thin-ring limit,
the current-curl operator selects the even radial angular projection \(\cos\vartheta\), while the
helicity normalization and BdG second-variation symmetrization each contribute a factor
\(1/2\). Hence
\[
\frac12_{\rm hel}\frac12_{\rm BdG}
\int_0^{2\pi}\cos^2\vartheta\,d\vartheta
=
\frac14\pi
=
\frac{\pi}{4}.
\]
Thus the projected chiral bridge coefficient is
\[
\lambda_\perp^{\rm BdG}
=
\frac{\pi}{4}
\left(\lambda {m_0\over\rho_0}\right)
\left(\frac{R_e}{\xi}\right)^2.
\]
Using \(R_e/\xi=1/\alpha\), this becomes the parameter-free result
\[
\boxed{\lambda_\perp^{\rm BdG}=\frac{\pi}{4}.}
\]

Numerically, inserting this value into the Hermitian helicity-projected BdG matrix gives
\[
\omega_{\rm hel}/\omega_c=0.2068070675,
\]
within \(9.3\times10^{-4}\) fractionally of the working muon target \(0.207\,\omega_c\). The
small residual is of the expected size for a leading thin-ring projection evaluated on a finite
grid and an unrelaxed wrapped straight-core background.

Open Problem 2 — Muon: coupled toroidal-chiral eigenmode

The muon assignment \(m_{\mu^\pm}=(3/2)\mu_0\) should not be regarded as derived until the
full 3D toroidal fluctuation problem has been solved. The planar LogSE breathing mode is
excluded analytically by Appendix C: its effective potential is everywhere repulsive and
supports no bound state. The required computation is therefore more specific than “find a muon
mode”. One must linearise the full theory \(\mathcal L+\mathcal L_\perp\) about the equilibrium
toroidal vortex, retain the full ring curvature, and solve for the lowest localized eigenmode
that mixes major-radius breathing with the transverse chiral channel.

The scale target is fixed in advance. The uncoupled ring-breathing estimate gives
\(\omega_{\rm ring}\approx 0.016\,\omega_c\), whereas the observed muon requires
\(\omega_\mu \approx 0.207\,\omega_c\) in the present normalization. The coupled toroidal
calculation must therefore produce an enhancement of order thirteen. In reduced form this is
the statement that the quadratic two-mode system
\[
(K_R-\omega^2 M_R)(K_\chi-\omega^2 M_\chi)-G^2=0
\]
must possess a lowest bound root at the muon scale. A successful solution would convert the
muon from an empirical ladder rung into a genuine eigenmode prediction of the theory.
