This note reformulates the open muon problem in `SSV-01-draft-07` as a concrete eigenvalue problem.

## 1. What is already established

From the draft:

- The bulk order parameter obeys the logarithmic Schrödinger equation
  \[
  i\hbar \partial_t \Psi
  =
  -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_0}\nabla^2 \Psi
  -
  b\rho_0 \ln\!\left(\frac{\rho_0 |\Psi|^2}{\bar\rho}\right)\Psi .
  \]

- The additional chiral-shear term in the Lagrangian is
  \[
  \mathcal L_\perp
  =
  -\frac{\lambda \hbar^2}{2m_0 \rho_0}\,(\nabla \times j)^2,
  \qquad
  j=\frac{\hbar}{2m_0 i}\left(\Psi^\ast \nabla \Psi-\Psi \nabla \Psi^\ast\right).
  \]

- The electron equilibrium torus has
  \[
  R_e=\frac{\xi}{\alpha}=\frac{\hbar}{\alpha m_e c},
  \qquad
  \xi=\frac{\hbar}{m_e c}.
  \]

- The ladder scale is
  \[
  \mu_0=\frac{m_e}{\alpha},
  \]
  so the phenomenological muon target is
  \[
  m_\mu \stackrel{?}{=} \frac{3}{2}\mu_0=\frac{3m_e}{2\alpha}.
  \]

Numerically,

\[
\mu_0 \approx 70.025251644\ \mathrm{MeV},
\qquad
\frac{3}{2}\mu_0 \approx 105.037877466\ \mathrm{MeV}.
\]

## 2. Why the current planar BdG calculation is insufficient

The draft already proves that the planar amplitude operator
\[
\hat H_{\rm amp}=-\partial_{\tilde s}^2+\frac{1}{\tilde s}\partial_{\tilde s}+4(1-f_0^2)
\]
has no bound s-wave breathing mode because
\[
V_{\rm eff}=4(1-f_0^2)\ge 0.
\]

So the muon cannot come from a planar core-pulsation mode. The only viable route left inside the draft is:

- full 3D toroidal geometry,
- a major-radius ring-breathing coordinate,
- coupling of that mode to the chiral transverse sector generated by `L_perp`.

## 3. Minimal collective-coordinate model

The cleanest first-principles target is to reduce the full field theory to two coupled collective coordinates:

- `q(t)` = fractional breathing of the torus major radius,
- `chi(t)` = amplitude of the lowest transverse chiral mode bound to the torus.

Write
\[
R(t)=R_e\,[1+q(t)] .
\]

Then expand the full action to quadratic order around the static torus:
\[
S^{(2)}=
\frac12\int dt
\left[
M_R \dot q^2
+M_\chi \dot\chi^2
-K_R q^2
-K_\chi \chi^2
-2G q\chi
\right].
\]

The corresponding normal-mode equation is
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
K_R-\omega^2 M_R & G \\
G & K_\chi-\omega^2 M_\chi
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
q\\
\chi
\end{pmatrix}
=0.
\]

So the coupled frequencies are determined by
\[
(K_R-\omega^2 M_R)(K_\chi-\omega^2 M_\chi)-G^2=0.
\]

This is the shortest route from the draft to a real muon derivation.

## 4. Bare ring mode

The draft already gives the uncoupled ring estimate
\[
\omega_{\rm ring}^2 \sim \alpha^2 \omega_c^2 \ln(1/\alpha),
\qquad
\omega_c=\frac{m_e c^2}{\hbar},
\]
so
\[
\frac{\omega_{\rm ring}}{\omega_c}
\sim
\alpha \sqrt{\ln(1/\alpha)}
\approx 0.016.
\]

This is too small for the muon, because the muon requires
\[
\frac{\omega_\mu}{\omega_c}
=
\frac{m_\mu}{m_e}
\approx 206.768.
\]

In the draft's rescaled comparison, that same target is quoted as
\[
\omega_\mu \approx 0.207\,\omega_c
\]
relative to the mode normalization used there, so the required enhancement is about
\[
\frac{0.207}{0.016}\approx 12.94.
\]

## 5. Full field-theory eigenproblem

To make the result genuinely first-principles, start from a toroidal background solution
\[
\Psi_0(\mathbf x)
=
f(\eta,\zeta)\,e^{i\Theta(\mathbf x)}
\]
written in local torus coordinates:

- `s` = minor-radius distance from the vortex core,
- `phi` = angle along the major circle,
- `vartheta` = poloidal angle around the core.

Then perturb
\[
\Psi(\mathbf x,t)
=
e^{-i\mu_{\rm chem} t/\hbar}
\left[
\Psi_0(\mathbf x)
+u(\mathbf x)e^{-i\omega t}
+v^\ast(\mathbf x)e^{i\omega t}
\right].
\]

Including the `L_perp` contribution, the quadratic fluctuation problem takes the form
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
\hat L_0+\hat L_\perp & \hat M_0+\hat M_\perp \\
-(\hat M_0+\hat M_\perp)^\ast & -(\hat L_0+\hat L_\perp)^\ast
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
u\\
v
\end{pmatrix}
=
\hbar\omega
\begin{pmatrix}
u\\
v
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

Here:

- `L_0, M_0` come from the LogSE part,
- `L_perp, M_perp` come from varying the chiral-shear energy to quadratic order,
- torus curvature enters through the 3D metric and background phase `Theta`.

The muon candidate is the lowest bound eigenmode with:

- azimuthally symmetric density change in the minor cross-section,
- nontrivial dependence on the major-radius coordinate,
- nonzero overlap with the transverse chiral sector.

## 6. Practical reduced ansatz

A useful projection ansatz is
\[
u(\mathbf x)=A\,\partial_R \Psi_0(\mathbf x)+B\,\Phi_\perp(\mathbf x),
\qquad
v(\mathbf x)=C\,\partial_R \Psi_0^\ast(\mathbf x)+D\,\Phi_\perp^\ast(\mathbf x),
\]
where:

- `partial_R Psi_0` captures the ring-breathing deformation,
- `Phi_perp` is the lowest localized transverse-chiral fluctuation around the torus.

Projecting the BdG operator onto this basis yields a finite-dimensional generalized eigenproblem
\[
\mathsf H_{\rm eff}\,X=\hbar\omega\,\mathsf N\,X,
\]
which directly computes the mode shift from the coupling.

## 7. What would count as a derivation

The framework can claim a genuine muon derivation if the following chain is completed without inserting `3/2` by hand:

1. Solve for the static toroidal background `Psi_0`.
2. Compute the quadratic fluctuation operator from `L + L_perp`.
3. Find the lowest localized coupled ring-plus-chiral eigenmode.
4. Show that its eigenfrequency satisfies
   \[
   \hbar\omega_\mu = m_\mu c^2
   \]
   with
   \[
   m_\mu \approx 105.658\ \mathrm{MeV}.
   \]
5. Only after that identify whether the result numerically equals
   \[
   \frac{3}{2}\mu_0
   \]
   or differs from it by the observed `0.59%`.

## 8. Most important conclusion

Inside the present draft, the muon mass is not yet derived. The derivation problem is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{Find the lowest bound eigenvalue of the 3D toroidal BdG operator built from } \mathcal L+\mathcal L_\perp .
}
\]

The simplest intermediate milestone is the two-mode matrix model
\[
(K_R-\omega^2 M_R)(K_\chi-\omega^2 M_\chi)-G^2=0,
\]
because it tells you exactly what quantity must be large enough to bridge the factor-of-13 gap:
the ring-chiral coupling `G`.
