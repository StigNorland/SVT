# Methodological Rhyme

The framework often reasons across scale, but it should not be described as assuming literal
repetition of structures at different magnitudes.

The better principle is:

> scales do not repeat; they rhyme

or more formally:

> SSV uses structural rhyme across scale as an inferential guide: not the repetition of things,
> but the recurrence of relational motifs.

This means:

- particles, black holes, galaxies, and cosmological phases need not be identical in content
- but they may exhibit recurring structural motifs
- the recurrence is analogical and relational, not one-to-one and literal

This principle is especially useful when resisting a naive "fractal universe" formulation while
keeping the deeper intuition that pattern may recur across scale without exact duplication.
